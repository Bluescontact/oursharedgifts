Post to the Village Market

Every submission carries both — an ask and an offer. Records live on the map for 72 hours.

Demonstration. This is a preview of how the Gift Circle works. Go ahead and fill it in and post — the flow is simulated, so nothing is sent, stored, or shared. When the circle goes live, a post like this becomes a real ask and offer on the map.

The market only works when everyone brings both sides. Tell us what you need this week, and what you can give. Either side can be small. Either side might get claimed; both might; neither might. You decide who fits, and either of you can decline cleanly.

Your zip code and what you wrote are public on the map. Your first name is public only if you provide one. Your contact information is held privately and revealed only to the person who claims your ask or offer. Both records expire 72 hours after posting; you can re-up via the link in your confirmation email.

About you

If you fill this in, your first name appears on the public map alongside your records. Use just your first name, a nickname, or leave it blank. The map can be screenshotted; Git history retains posts.

How far you'd travel to give or receive — applies to both your ask and your offer.

Held privately. Revealed only to the matched party when someone claims one of your records.

What you need (Ask)

Up to 600 characters. Avoid details that would identify you (street, distinctive landmarks, full name). Let what you write be what you'd say to a stranger on the phone.

What you can give (Offer)

Up to 600 characters. The offer doesn't need to "match" your ask in any way. What's easy for you to give is what counts.

What happens after you submit

  1. Both records appear on the map at your zip's center within a few minutes (after the site rebuilds).
  2. You get a confirmation email with a link to manage your submission (withdraw either record, re-up the expiry, view status).
  3. When someone claims one of your records, you get a notification with their contact info; they get yours.
  4. Either of you can decline cleanly. No ratings, no records of refusals.
  5. Records expire 72 hours after posting unless you re-up. Withdraw any time via your management link.

If you want to take a record down before someone claims it, use your management link. If you've lost it, contact via the request page.
