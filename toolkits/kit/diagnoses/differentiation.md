# Diagnosis — Differentiation

Your weakest vertex is **Differentiation** — the capacity to know what you are and what you are not.

This rarely feels like a problem from the inside. It feels like flexibility, like responsiveness, like a group that meets needs as they arise. The cost is hidden in friction: every decision requires negotiation that genuine clarity would have made unnecessary. Trust can't form because nothing is distinct enough to trust.

The pattern most likely operating is **The Transaction Trap.** When no one is sure what anyone actually brings or actually needs, the system defaults to rigid contracts, score-keeping, and documentation as a substitute for trust.

The work is to make distinct again what has been blurred — not by adding rules, but by naming what was already true.
