# Before You Begin

This is a diagnostic, not a teaching. It assumes you are already responsible for a system whose coherence has begun to slip, and that you can answer the twelve questions about *that specific system* rather than in general. If you are new to the framework, read [The Meta-Tetrahedron](/library/meta-tetrahedron/) first — the eight-part operational lens. If you are unsure whether you have a specific system in mind, the audit is not ready yet. Come back when one is naming itself.

## The pre-audit gate

Before the questions begin, three statements need to be true. Answer for yourself, not aloud, not for anyone else.

1. I have a specific system in mind — a team, a relationship, a project, a body, a protocol, a community.
2. The system has begun to feel incoherent in some way that I cannot fully name.
3. I am responsible for or inside the system, not auditing it from outside.

If any of the three is false, the audit waits. Sit with what isn't ready. The work is not to push the gate; the work is to let it become true.
