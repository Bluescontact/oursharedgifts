# Connection × Institutional

## Signature

Information flows constantly. Meetings happen on schedule. Communications channels are full. And yet, decisions made at one level routinely surprise the level above and below; field signals do not change policy; alignment meetings produce documents that nobody acts on. The shape of organizational contact is intact; the contact itself is not occurring.

## Reading

Movement 2 + **Two_Living_Systems.docx**. Two living systems in genuine contact change each other in observable ways. Two institutional layers in genuine connection produce traceable effects on one another's decisions. Without the trace, the connection is a story the institution is telling itself.

## Practice

Run a **Gift Alignment Event** with the team. The structured format makes the difference between performed exchange and actual exchange visible immediately.

## Intervention

Cancel one recurring meeting. Replace it with a single 20-minute conversation where the only agenda is what each person actually needs to ask or offer that week. The withdrawal is from the standing-meeting habit. The gift is the conversation format that requires arrival rather than attendance.

## Expected resistance

*"But how will we stay aligned?"* — You weren't, and the meeting was where the appearance of alignment was being maintained. The new format will reveal where alignment was performed and where it was real.

## Signal of success

A piece of information from one part of the institution measurably changes a decision in another part within a week. The change can be traced and named.
