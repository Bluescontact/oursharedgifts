# Boundaries × Relational

## Signature

You are doing the emotional labor of the relationship — naming the patterns, holding the repairs, tracking what's happening between you. You are also exhausted. The other person seems unaware of the labor, or aware and not absorbing it. Your resentment is rising. You blame yourself for resenting. The cycle continues because the labor cannot be paused without something visible breaking.

## Reading

Movement 3 + **Green Hand.odt**. Boundaries in living relationship are not walls but membranes — they let some things in and not others, and they tell you which is which.

## Practice

**24 Affirmations: Boundaries and Vulnerability** — alone first, until they land in the body. Then with the relevant other, when the body can hold its own ground.

## Intervention

Identify one capacity you have been performing past your actual limit. Tell the relevant person: *"I have been doing X past my actual capacity. I am naming the limit. Here is what I can actually offer."* The withdrawal is from the over-performance; the gift is the spoken sentence, calm, without apology, that tells them where the line actually is.

## Expected resistance

*"They will hear it as withdrawal of love."* — They might. The framing is theirs to make; the line is yours to hold. Love that requires you to perform past capacity is love that has been routed through depletion. What you are returning to is love that does not.

## Signal of success

A boundary you state stays stated. You do not have to restate it weekly. The other person begins to meet you at the named limit rather than past it.
