# Boundaries × Institutional

## Signature

One role is doing the work of two or three. That person is exhausted, possibly heroic, possibly resentful. The institution depends on their over-performance and has adapted to it. When they take a vacation, the cracks show; when they leave permanently, the institution discovers it never had the capacity it appeared to have.

## Reading

Movement 3 + **doc3_metacognitive_exchange.docx**. Metacognitive exchange names the layer of awareness that allows a system to notice its own boundary violations. Institutional Boundaries require this layer to function at scale; without it, every boundary violation is invisible to the institution while obvious to the body absorbing it.

## Practice

**voice-expression.html** — practice naming what you will and will not do. Walk this in the personal register first; institutional boundaries are spoken by people, and the speaking has to be available before it can scale.

## Intervention

Identify one bad-news item the group has been softening or hiding. State it plainly in the next meeting, as information, not failure. The withdrawal is from the protection-of-leadership-from-reality pattern; the gift is the statement of fact that lets the institution see itself.

## Expected resistance

*"That will create panic."* — Panic is what the suppression has been deferring. Naming reality earlier produces smaller panics, sooner, with more time to respond.

## Signal of success

A capacity gap that was previously hidden is named in a meeting, and the response is structural change rather than escalation of the over-performance that was hiding it.
