# Differentiation × Relational

## Signature

You answer "what do you want for dinner?" with "what do you want?" — not occasionally, as a courtesy, but reflexively, because you cannot locate your own preference until you know theirs. Your moods track theirs without conscious effort. Their stress becomes your stress before you have time to choose. Inside the relationship, you know yourself less reliably than you do alone.

## Reading

Movement 1 + **Un-spelling Separation.pdf**. Separation is the failure mode, and the cure is not isolation but distinction — the capacity to be two people inside the same room, the same conversation, the same intimacy.

## Practice

**self-recognition.html**, with a partner. Each person reads their list aloud to the other, slowly, without commentary. Hearing your own distinct contributions out loud, witnessed, is the practice.

## Intervention

In your next disagreement, hold your distinct position before negotiating toward shared ground. Do not give it up to keep the peace; do not perform conviction you do not feel. Let the difference be in the room as long as it actually is. The withdrawal is from premature merging; the gift is the differentiated self that made the merging worth doing in the first place.

## Expected resistance

*"But we're so close — won't this create distance?"* — The closeness was being maintained at the cost of one of you. The distance you are afraid of is the space where two distinct people can actually meet.

## Signal of success

You can disagree with the other person, hold your position through the disagreement, and feel closer afterward rather than further apart.
