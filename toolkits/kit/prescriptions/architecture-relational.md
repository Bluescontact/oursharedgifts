# Architecture × Relational

## Signature

You are doing for the other person something they could now do for themselves. Maybe you always could; maybe they have grown into the capacity while you were still meeting the original need. Your continuing to do it is preventing them from developing — and binding you into a role that has outlasted its purpose. Both of you sense it. Neither has named it.

## Reading

Movement 4 + **Essence of the Gift (refined).pdf**. The gift that prevents growth is the gift named in the original framework as the most painful to recognize: it was generosity, then it became infrastructure, then it became a ceiling.

## Practice

**developmental-geometry.html**. Walk it in the relationship's specific terms: what shape has the capacity-sharing taken, and what shape would let both people develop further?

## Intervention

Identify one thing you keep doing for the other person that they could now do for themselves. Stop. Tell them why, plainly: *"I have been doing this, and I am stopping, because I think you can do it now and my doing it is in the way."* Leave the tool, template, or resource at the threshold — whatever they need to take it on. Do not rescue them when they struggle; the struggle is the architecture forming.

## Expected resistance

*"But they need this from me."* — They needed it. The need has changed and the offering hasn't. Generosity that has not updated to the receiver's actual current state is no longer a gift; it is a maintenance of the past.

## Signal of success

The other person picks up the capacity, with stumbling, then with competence. Your relationship has more space in it. Both of you can do something now that neither of you could do before this move.
