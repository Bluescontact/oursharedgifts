# Architecture × Institutional

## Signature

One person is the institutional memory. They onboarded everyone informally; they hold the unwritten rules; they know which exceptions matter and which don't. The organization cannot articulate how it works without them. When they take a vacation, decisions queue up. When they leave, the institution has to be rediscovered.

## Reading

Movement 4 + Phase 3 of **tools/tetrahedral-audit.html** (Strategic Intervention). The audit's own Phase 3 is a worksheet for institutional Architecture: identify the dependency, describe the gift to leave at the threshold, name the expected crisis, specify the new capacity that must develop.

## Practice

**My Menu of Gifts.odt** for each member; share at the next gathering. The exercise makes individual capacity legible, which is the first step toward making it transferable.

## Intervention

Identify one thing only the most central person can currently do. Document it well enough that someone else can do it. Withdraw. Leave the document at the threshold. The crisis that follows is the architecture forming — do not rescue it.

## Expected resistance

*"It would be faster to just keep doing it."* — Every time you keep doing it, you are paying interest on a debt the institution is carrying. The cost is invisible until you are unavailable, and then the cost is the institution.

## Signal of success

A new contributor can do useful work in their first month using only the documented artifacts, without informal onboarding from the previously irreplaceable person.
