# Architecture × Biological

## Signature

Your body's capacity is being maintained by a single intervention — one practice, one supplement, one relationship that regulates your nervous system, one ritual without which the wheels come off. Remove the intervention and you can feel the capacity collapse. The body is not running its own architecture; the intervention is running the body.

## Reading

Movement 4 + **`ecological_succession_lens.md`**. Succession creates soil that enables future complexity. Architecture in the biological substrate is what compounds across days rather than what carries each day's load alone.

## Practice

**My Menu of Gifts.odt** — but inverted: inventory the practices, conditions, and supports your body actually relies on, and mark which ones are *building capacity* versus which ones are *renting it*.

## Intervention

Identify one daily expenditure that doesn't accumulate — energy spent that has to be re-spent tomorrow with no compounding effect. Replace it with one structural change to your environment, schedule, or pattern that makes tomorrow's version unnecessary. The withdrawal is from the daily-effort mode. The gift is the modified environment that does the work for you while you sleep.

## Expected resistance

*"But the practice works."* — Yes. That is exactly the trap. A practice that works keeps you returning to do it; a structural change works while you do something else.

## Signal of success

One thing that used to require daily effort no longer requires it, and the capacity it produced has not decreased.
