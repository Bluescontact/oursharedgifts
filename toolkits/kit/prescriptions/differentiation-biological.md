# Differentiation × Biological

## Signature

You struggle to answer "what am I feeling?" without thinking about it. By the time the answer arrives, the feeling has changed or the body has moved on. You know what's happening in the room more reliably than what's happening in your tissue. You catch other people's moods before you catch your own.

## Reading

Movement 1 + **`somatic_practices_lens.md`**. The somatic lens names the body's prior knowledge — that the nervous system distinguishes self from stimulus before conscious thought arrives. Differentiation in this substrate is recovery of that prior knowledge.

## Practice

**self-recognition.html**, daily, two weeks. Read aloud, slowly, with attention to where in the body each affirmation lands or doesn't.

## Intervention

For one week, do not narrate any sensation. Notice it; do not name it; do not story it. The story is what has been substituted for the sensation. Without the story, the sensation completes its own arc. The withdrawal is from the cognitive translation layer; the gift is the felt completion the body had not been allowed to finish.

## Expected resistance

*"But how will I know what I'm feeling?"* — You will know exactly the way the body has been telling you all along. The question itself is the substitution layer working hard to remain necessary.

## Signal of success

A sensation begins, runs its course, and ends, and you can locate it in your body before you can label it.
