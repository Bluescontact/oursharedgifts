# Boundaries × Biological

## Signature

Your body's *no* arrives late, quietly, or only as crisis. You finish the meal before noticing you were full. You complete the task before noticing you were exhausted. The signals exist; they have been overridden so consistently that they no longer try to interrupt — they wait for collapse.

## Reading

Movement 3 + **`somatic_practices_lens.md`** (the body's *no*). The body's boundary system is older and faster than the conscious mind. When it has gone quiet, it has not stopped speaking; the receiver has stopped listening.

## Practice

**24 Affirmations: Boundaries and Vulnerability** — alone, slowly, several times. Note which lines the body affirms and which the body cannot yet receive. The ones it cannot yet receive are the boundary information.

## Intervention

Name one limit your body has been telling you about that you have been overriding. Honor it for one week. Do not make a rule; honor the specific signal. The withdrawal is from the override habit. The gift is the felt experience of the body being met by its own reception, possibly for the first time in a long time.

## Expected resistance

*"The limit isn't really there; I'm being weak."* — The framing of weak versus strong is what the override mechanism uses to recruit consent. The body does not call its limits weak. It calls them limits.

## Signal of success

A limit begins to register in time to be honored, rather than after collapse has done the honoring.
