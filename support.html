Consulting

Structural Recognition

A diagnostic pattern-recognition analysis of your project, organization, or initiative. Kevin spends time with your documentation, your people, and your conditions — then produces a structural recognition document that names what's present, what's developing, and where to build next.

This isn't generic consulting. It's the application of the tetrahedral diagnostic to your specific context.

What you get: A written structural analysis (typically 3–5 pages) identifying current vertex strengths, the weakest capacity, specific failure patterns, and concrete practices for development. One practitioner, one project, sustained attention.

How this sustains itself

The cycle

The framework describes gift economy as operating through overflow — the system that maintains itself coherently generates surplus, and the surplus is the gift. But overflow requires that self-maintenance is happening. If the practitioner is depleted, nothing flows.

These engagement paths form a closed loop: structural recognition consulting generates revenue and portfolio evidence. Evidence generates applicants for facilitated programs. Programs produce practitioners who use the framework. Practitioners generate visibility. Visibility generates consulting inquiries. Each cycle produces documentation that enters commons — the free tier grows every time the paid tier operates.

The boundary is clean. The pattern is gift. The practice carries a price. Both are true. Neither compromises the other.
