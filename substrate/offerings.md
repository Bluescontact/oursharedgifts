# `offerings.html` — Gift Menu

## Purpose
The catalog of what Kevin offers in the **gift zone** — what's available, in what shape, with what limits, in which Wheel of Consent quadrant. The receiver browses here to understand whether what they need is something Kevin can give.

## Role in the architecture
**Differentiation vertex.** The page where the system declares what it is by declaring what it offers and what it does not. Per *Theory of Gift* Axis 2 (Self-Knowledge): a boundary stated as information opens; a boundary stated as confrontation closes.

## Must do
- List each offering as a **card with three parts**:
  1. **What is offered.** Specific, time-bounded, named.
  2. **What is NOT offered.** The negative space. Not a disclaimer — a structural fact. (e.g., for *Witnessing*: "No fixing. No advice unless you ask.")
  3. **Quadrant.** Which Wheel of Consent quadrant this lives in (Serving / Allowing / Taking / Accepting). Most will be Serving (Kevin doing, receiver benefiting). A few may be Accepting (Kevin receiving — e.g., learning a skill).
- Carry forward the offerings from the [gift menu PDF](../../Downloads/gift menu-1-1.pdf): Cleaning, Organizing, Building, Thinking partner, Partner dance instruction, Witnessing.
- Include the line: *"If what you need isn't here, ask anyway. The list is a starting point, not a fence."*
- Each card links to or surfaces the request flow — but not as a "Book Now" button. As "Ask about this."

## Must NOT do
- **No prices.** Not even "free" labeled prominently — "free" frames the offering as the absence of a price, which keeps the exchange-economy frame in play. The frame here is gift, not free.
- **No tip jars, no "support Kevin" links on cards.** Donation rail lives only on `support.html`, separated from the offerings.
- **No urgency.** No "limited slots this week." No scarcity framing. Capacity is a real limit but it's communicated in the request flow, not as marketing.
- **No social proof per-card.** No "5 people received cleaning this month." That converts the offerings into a marketplace listing.
- **No upsells to consulting.** The exchange tier lives elsewhere. A receiver looking at gift offerings should not be routed toward paid services.

## Failure modes
- **Over-listing.** If the menu grows past ~8 offerings, it stops being a menu and becomes a service catalog — receiver browses comparatively rather than recognizing what fits.
- **Vague offerings.** "Help with stuff" is unbounded; the receiver's nervous system registers this as either too-good-to-be-true or as an open trap. Specific, bounded offerings let the body relax.
- **Hiding the limits.** If the "what is NOT offered" line is in small print or footnoted, the limit becomes apology rather than information. Equal weight to both.

## Open questions
- Should "Witnessing" appear above or below the more conventional offerings (Cleaning / Organizing / Building)? Above signals it's not buried; below lets the page warm up first. Probably above with a thin divider.
- One page or one card per offering with deeper detail? Probably one page — depth on a card crosses into selling.
- Quadrant labels visible to receivers, or only used internally to design the cards? Visible adds vocabulary; invisible keeps the page lighter. Lean toward invisible at v1 with a small footnote linking to the framework.
