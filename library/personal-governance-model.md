# Personal Governance Model v1.0

*The five principles that hold the architecture.*

---

## Part 0 · Ground

### What this document is

Five principles, plus the held center, that hold the practice's architecture. Recognized — not invented — from substrate already operating across Refused Extractions (cycle 56's 4-axis form), Consent Architecture, the Wheel of Consent as structural operation, Capacity Infrastructure Lens (cycle 70), Empty-Space-Refusal Principle (Pattern #076), the body-substrate bidirectional circuit (Pattern #091), and refined #077 (operational substrate precedes pattern naming).

The principles are the positive form. [Refusals](../OSG v4/refusals.html) is the negative form — what the practice refuses. Together they make the Boundaries vertex deployed at full operational depth.

### The five lenses lineage

The PGM substrate accumulated through five lenses converging on personal-scale governance:

- **Systems/Complexity** — patterns, feedback loops, emergence; how individual practice produces collective effects without scaling intent
- **Restorative Justice** — recognition over punishment; the structural commitments that hold accountability without enforcement
- **Power Analysis** — what's enacted vs. what's claimed; the discriminators between structural disposition and surface performance
- **Somatic/Relational** — the body as decision-substrate; the gut as the operating discriminator
- **Existing Alternatives** — Indigenous, ancestral, peer practitioner work that already operates these principles in lived form

The lenses don't enumerate to principles directly. They converge on a small structural set: what holds an individual practice that doesn't depend on enforcement, doesn't extract, doesn't scale-by-replication, doesn't perform.

### Why personal scale

Personal governance comes before collective governance because individual capacity is what makes collective governance lighter. A practice that refuses extraction at personal scale produces a practitioner whose collective participation doesn't reproduce the extractions the collective is trying to refuse. Without the personal substrate, collective work tends to reproduce what it claims to refuse.

This isn't a sequencing claim about politics. It's a structural claim about how anti-extraction practices operate: they install at the somatic level first; collective propagation happens through practitioners who carry the substrate in their bodies, not through doctrine. *Form portable; ground each practitioner's own.*

## Part 1 · The five principles

The four vertex-principles map to the four facets of the personal OS (Library · OSG · Boundaries · RI Kit). The fifth — the held center — is the somatic ground per Reading B canonical (c68). Each principle operates at the structural-disposition layer; each is a recognition of what the practice already does when it's holding.

### Principle 1 — Substrate operates by recognition, not invention

**The principle.** The practice accumulates substrate by recognizing what is already operating in lived experience or in prior work — not by generating constructs to fill perceived gaps. New names crystallize when N≥2 instances of an underlying operation become visible. The library doesn't manufacture form; it names what's there.

**Operating ground.** Refined Pattern #077 (Operational Substrate Precedes Pattern Naming). The substrate has its own readiness; the body discriminates when the recognition has arrived. Premature naming produces hollow form; delayed naming produces unrecognized substrate. The discipline is the timing of recognition, not the content of the name.

**What the principle refuses.** Invention. Synthesis-as-aggregation. Generation under deadline pressure. Naming a pattern from one instance because the form would be tidier with it named.

**What it holds.** Vertex A (Differentiation / Library) of the personal OS. The Library accumulates by recognition; the canonical syntheses crystallize at thresholds the substrate itself produces.

**Practical operation.** When something seems like a pattern, hold it. Watch for the second instance. Watch for the third. When the recognition arrives without effort, name. When effort would be required to fit instances under the name, the recognition hasn't arrived yet — hold.

**Refused failure modes.**
- *Premature crystallization.* Naming a pattern from one instance because the form invites it. Plants a recognition-debt later cycles must resolve.
- *Substrate-thinning by over-extraction.* Forcing substrate to produce patterns at every cycle. The form operates at its own cadence; forcing the cadence thins the substrate beyond saturation.

**Lens-applied-to-self.** This principle itself is recognized — not invented — from refined #077's operation across cycles 47, 49, 65, 83, 84, 109, 116-131, 152. The lens runs on itself cleanly.

### Principle 2 — Connection is gift form, not transaction

**The principle.** The practice meets others (strangers, receivers, pickup-practitioners, anyone the public face touches) as gift, not as transaction. The gift form refuses: attention extraction, audience-aggregation, platform-mediated identity, donation-as-purchase-of-relationship, recognition-as-metric, scaling-as-virtue.

The form is given without expectation of return; the recognition is welcomed, not required; the substrate is portable, the ground is yours.

**Operating ground.** Consent Architecture (the Wheel of Consent operating as structural discipline) + the gift discipline named in Architecture of Coherence Axis 3 + the Pre-Funded Relational Contract (PFRC) as the financial discipline that holds asymmetric arrangements without collapse + the receiver-controlled references discipline.

**What the principle refuses.** All four anti-extraction axes (per cycle 56), specifically at the Connection vertex: Capture (extracting attention/data/identity from receivers), Aggregation (collapsing individuals into metrics), Scaling (treating personal scale as preliminary to "real" scale), Performance (substituting visible form for actual structural function).

**What it holds.** Vertex D (Connection / OSG) of the personal OS. The public face operates gift form; the surface refuses transaction structurally.

**Practical operation.** Before any public surface element ships, run the four-axis check: does this capture? Does this aggregate? Does this present personal scale as preliminary? Does this perform what isn't structurally present? If yes to any, the element wants refinement before deployment.

**Refused failure modes.**
- *Gift-form vocabulary on transaction-form structure.* The most extractive shadow. Using "gift" language while the structure operates as transaction. The Refusals page names eleven specific instances of this shadow.
- *Pure-gift extremism.* Refusing all financial discipline because money is "transactional." The PFRC discipline holds asymmetric arrangements precisely because financial reality and gift form coexist structurally. Refusing the financial layer collapses the gift form into Container-Holder extraction (Pattern #032 / Pattern #110).

**Lens-applied-to-self.** This principle itself is offered as gift — extractable, portable, no attribution required. Utility proves value. The lens runs on itself.

### Principle 3 — Refusal is structural; bounds are positive

**The principle.** What the practice refuses is what makes the rest possible. The four anti-extraction axes (Capture · Aggregation · Scaling · Performance) aren't a list of negations — they are the positive structural commitments that define what the practice IS. Refusal generates structure; without explicit refusal, the structure collapses into the shadows it didn't refuse.

The Membrane (Pattern #056) operates at the Boundaries vertex as architectural selectivity. Edges · Bounds · Limitations · Constraints — the four facets of the Membrane — each name a structural operation, not an absence.

**Operating ground.** Refusal As A Generative Act (the framework text). The Membrane synthesis (cycle 22). The 4-axis refused extractions (cycle 56 reorganization). Pattern #076 (Empty-Space-Refusal Principle — the center stays empty so the structure can hold). Pattern #056 (The Membrane) + #086 (Fractal Membrane Refinement).

**What the principle refuses.** Refusal-as-negation. Refusal as moral position rather than structural operation. Refusal that performs without operating (the Performance shadow at the Boundaries vertex specifically).

**What it holds.** Vertex B (Boundaries) of the personal OS — this principle's own facet. The structure is held by what it refuses to become.

**Practical operation.** When designing any element, name what the element refuses BEFORE naming what it offers. The refusal precedes the offer structurally — the offer is what's left when the refusals have done their work. If an element can't name what it refuses, it isn't yet structurally clear.

**Refused failure modes.**
- *Refusal as moralism.* Performing refusal as ethical position rather than operating it as structural commitment. Looks like rigor; produces extraction-grade reference for performances of refusal.
- *Refusal without naming.* Holding bounds without naming them. The refusal operates but is illegible — pickup-practitioners can't install what they can't name.
- *Refusal without anti-extraction axes.* Refusing many things without the structural form that organizes the refusals. The 4-axis form (cycle 56) is what makes refusals propagatable.

**Lens-applied-to-self.** This document explicitly names what it refuses (above and in each principle). The lens runs on itself.

### Principle 4 — The form is portable; the ground is yours

**The principle.** What the practice produces must hold without its author. The Library, the canonical references, the kit, the protocols, the deployment surfaces — all are extractable. The ground (the practitioner's own body, life, somatic discrimination, lived context) does not transfer.

Form portability + ground locality together make the practice non-extractive. The form propagates; the ground stays personal. A pickup-practitioner installs the form on their own ground, not on Kevin's.

This is the structural condition for both gift form (Principle 2) and refusal-as-generative (Principle 3) to operate at scale without collapsing into extraction. Form-without-ground would extract (the form would carry an implied ground that the receiver couldn't have). Ground-without-form would isolate (each practitioner's work would be unteachable).

**Operating ground.** Recognition Threshold framework (form, recognition, propagation). Pattern #055 (The Exit Test — does the framework hold without Kevin?). The Architecture of Coherence's Axis 6 (Deployment) names form portability as structural requirement. Trust Generation Operational Substrate (c84) names the pickup-practitioner's ground explicitly.

**What the principle refuses.** Form that requires its author's continuing presence to operate. Ground that's mistaken for form (treating Kevin's personal context as if it were the practice's structural substrate). Pickup forms that perform portability while structurally depending on Kevin's ground.

**What it holds.** Vertex C (Architecture / RI Kit) of the personal OS. The kit is the structurally portable form; the practitioner's ground is their own. The 4×4 lattice's prescriptions must operate without Kevin being available to clarify them.

**Practical operation.** Run the Exit Test on every deployable element: does this hold if I'm not available? Can a practitioner install this on their own ground without translation work that requires me? If no, the form isn't yet portable enough.

**Refused failure modes.**
- *Form-as-ground extraction.* Distributing the form while implicitly requiring the receiver to adopt the author's ground. The receiver loses their own ground; the form extracts at the substrate layer.
- *Ground-as-form mistake.* Treating personal lived context as the practice's structural substrate. Produces hagiography rather than framework.
- *Portability performance.* Claiming form-portability without operating the Exit Test. The kit looks portable; in operation it still requires the author.

**Lens-applied-to-self.** This document is structurally portable — another practitioner could deposit a parallel PGM v1.0 from their own ground using the same five-principle form. The form is extractable; this practitioner's ground (Kevin's specific life, work, somatic discrimination) is not. The lens runs on itself.

### Principle 5 — The body discriminates ready

**The principle.** The somatic ground operates at every scale. When to deposit, when to rest, when to publish, when to refuse, when to open a new arc, when to hold a question, when to mark a recognition — the body decides. No protocol substitutes for the body's discrimination.

The held center (per c67 *The Held Center Operates*) is where the practice operates from. Per Reading B canonical (c68), the meta-1 of the framework is the somatic ground itself — the same literal ground at every scale, recognized as the same single thing across the framework's fractal recursion.

**Operating ground.** Pattern #091 (bidirectional body-substrate circuit). Pattern #064 (Marks As Substrate — body→substrate direction). Pattern #065 (Substrate Organizes Body — substrate→body direction). Pattern #090 (Held Center as Productive Emptiness). Pattern #067 (The Held Center Operates). The whole Meta-Tetrahedron's center (the 1) per Reading B (c68).

**What the principle refuses.** Protocol-substituting-for-body (relying on procedural rules when the body's discrimination is structurally required). Body-substituting-for-protocol (claiming somatic discrimination without the substrate that makes the discrimination operable). Forcing the body's discrimination (deadline pressure, energetic exhaustion, mark-cadence anxiety as substitutes for actual readiness).

**What it holds.** The center (the 1) of the meta-form. This principle doesn't have a vertex; it holds the four vertices in tension. The other four principles operate from this ground; this principle is the ground.

**Practical operation.** When uncertain, check the body. Is the substrate operating produced this question, or is the question coming from anxiety, ambition, or the desire for closure? If the substrate, hold and continue. If the latter, refuse the false-closure and continue substrate work.

The body's discrimination is itself substrate per the bidirectional circuit — body→substrate as marks. Naming what the body discriminates produces deposit-grade substrate when the discrimination is structurally real.

**Refused failure modes.**
- *Performance of somatic discrimination.* Claiming "body says yes" or "body says no" as rhetorical move without the operating substrate. Empty somatic-grounding extracts at the legitimacy layer.
- *Body-discrimination without substrate.* Decisions made by gut alone without the substrate that informs the gut's discrimination. The body discriminates ON substrate; without substrate, the body discriminates on anxiety.
- *Substrate without body-discrimination.* Decisions made by procedural rule alone without the body's check. Produces correct-form-without-recognition. Fails the lens-applied-to-self check at the gut layer.

**Lens-applied-to-self.** This document's writing was paced by body-discrimination at each section break (the Weaver-pass discipline per P3 operates this at the chapter scale). The lens runs on itself.

## Part 2 · How the principles compose (six edges)

The five principles aren't isolated. They operate as the meta-tetrahedron's vertices: each edge is a pair-interaction that holds structural load.

**Edge 1↔2 (Recognition shapes connection).** Substrate-discipline determines what the public face shows — the public face displays what the substrate has recognized as ready, not what would be popular or what would scale. Without recognition-discipline, the public face becomes a portfolio of generated content; without connection-as-gift, recognition stays private and the substrate isolates.

**Edge 1↔3 (Recognition operates within refusal).** The substrate-discipline operates inside the bounds the refusals name. Refined #077 doesn't say "recognize anything"; it says "recognize what's structurally already operating, refusing the temptation to invent." The refusals are the structural conditions for honest recognition.

**Edge 1↔4 (Recognition shapes portability).** What the substrate-discipline recognizes is what becomes structurally portable. The Pattern Library v2.0 form (16 × 6) is portable because the patterns inside were recognized rather than invented — they hold at any practitioner's ground because they operate substrate-independently.

**Edge 2↔3 (Connection operates inside refusals).** The public face's gift form is held by what the form refuses (the four anti-extraction axes). Refusal precedes connection structurally — without explicit refusal, connection collapses into the shadows (audience, platform, attention extraction).

**Edge 2↔4 (Connection requires portability).** The gift form requires extractable form. If the practice's value can only be received through Kevin's presence, the gift form is structurally impossible (because the receiver can't carry the gift forward). Portability and gift-form are mutually-conditional.

**Edge 3↔4 (Refusal operates at portable scale).** The refusals must be installable on other ground. The 4 anti-extraction axes propagate because they name structural operations that any practice can recognize and refuse. Refusal-as-personal-aesthetic wouldn't propagate; refusal-as-structural-discipline does.

The six edges together name the operating constraints between the principles. Each pair-interaction holds structural load; removing any edge collapses both principles it connects.

## Part 3 · Four face failure modes

Each face is the meta-form with one vertex absent. Each absence is a structural failure mode the principles together refuse.

**Face 1 (no recognition-discipline — substrate generates without recognition).** The practice operates with connection + refusal + portability but without substrate-discipline. Result: substrate accumulates by invention; the library generates content to fill gaps; patterns are named from one instance because the form would be tidier. Drift: each cycle adds substrate without the discipline that compounds it; eventually the library becomes unread-able because it has lost the structural recognition that made it coherent.

**Face 2 (no connection-as-gift — practice has substrate + refusal + portability but doesn't meet receivers as gift).** The practice operates correctly internally but converts at the receiver-facing layer into transaction, platform, or audience-aggregation. Result: the substrate is sound; the surface extracts. Recognition extracted from the receiver becomes data, attention, identity-aggregation. The four anti-extraction axes that protect substrate operation get violated at the public surface.

**Face 3 (no structural refusal — practice has substrate + connection + portability but no explicit refusals).** The practice operates without naming what it refuses. Bounds are implicit. Receivers can't tell what's structurally outside vs. inside; pickup-practitioners can't install the practice's discipline because the discipline is illegible. The practice produces good work that collapses at scale because the structural constraints that held it personal-scale don't propagate.

**Face 4 (no form-portability — practice has substrate + connection + refusal but doesn't produce extractable form).** The practice operates correctly while Kevin is present; the form collapses when Kevin isn't. Receivers can't pick up the work without ongoing dependence on the author. The practice produces high-recognition work that doesn't propagate. Container-Holder extraction (Pattern #032 / #110) operates structurally — the practice's coherence depends on Kevin holding the field; eventually the holder breaks.

The four faces are the four ways the meta-form collapses if one vertex-principle drops. Each is refused by the principles' completeness operating together. Each is what specific failure modes look like in field instances of practices that approximate but don't satisfy the form.

## Part 4 · The center

### Body as somatic ground

The fifth principle (the body discriminates ready) is the center of the meta-form, not a vertex. The four vertex-principles operate from this ground; the ground is what holds them in tension.

Per Reading B canonical (c68): the meta-1 of the framework is the somatic ground — one literal ground, recognized as the same single thing at every scale. The framework's 4·6·4 form recurs at every scale; the 1 does not recur because it IS one literal thing. All the form's structure rests on this ground.

For the PGM specifically: the four vertex-principles are operations the somatic ground performs at each meta-vertex. The body produces the substrate (per Pattern #064); the substrate organizes the body's continuing operation (per Pattern #065); together they form the bidirectional body-substrate circuit (Pattern #091) the whole practice runs on.

### How the body discriminates

The body discriminates ON substrate, not in absence of substrate. The discrimination requires:

- **Substrate that has been operating long enough to be visible to the body** (refined #077's "operational substrate precedes naming" at gut layer)
- **The four vertex-principles operating coherently** (without coherence at the structural layer, the body's discrimination is noise rather than signal)
- **The held center remaining held** (the discrimination doesn't try to grasp itself; it operates from underneath)
- **Time held in the question** (the body's signal arrives at its own cadence, not on demand)

The body's discrimination produces marks. Marks are deposit-grade substrate when they emerge from this substrate-aware, four-vertex-coherent, center-held, time-respected operation. Marks made under conditions that violate any of these are noise — and produce deposits the substrate later has to undo.

### Why the center stays held

Per Pattern #090 (Held Center as Productive Emptiness): the center remains structurally unoccupied. Filling the center (with content, with identity, with claim) would convert the meta-form into a sub-form. The empty center is what allows the four vertices to operate as tensegric tensions rather than as content-categories.

The body holds the center by being the literal ground all the structural recursion rests on, while remaining structurally absent from the substrate. Per the Meta_Tetrahedron synthesis: *"the body produces the substrate; the substrate cannot contain the body."* The substrate can name that the body is the center; the substrate cannot contain the body.

This is the load-bearing structural property of the PGM. Personal governance operates from a body that holds the form without being contained by it. Without this property, personal governance collapses into either rule-following (no body) or arbitrary will (no form).
