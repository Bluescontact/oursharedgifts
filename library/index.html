The deep end

Library

The theory under the life. Full strength, on purpose.

Everything out front — the Model, the Refusals, the tools — stands on a body of written structure. This shelf is that structure, whole. The documents here use their own exact vocabulary and don't apologize for it; this index tells you in plain terms what each one is, who it's for, and what it asks of you, so you can pick the right door and skip the rest. Everything is a plain file — read it here, download it, keep it. Nothing is gated.

You don't have to read any of this to ask me for something or to run the model on your own ground. Start with the title that made you pause.

The canon — June 2026

  • The Architecture of Coherence — Integrated Edition · June 2026 · ~16pp One document written for three readers at once — the evaluator who wants the falsifiable core, the practitioner who wants the diagnostic, the operator who wants the governance stack. Built the way the framework is built: one pattern, true at every depth. The place to start, whoever you are. Asks one sitting.
  • The Architecture of Coherence — The Complete Suite · June 2026 · 118pp Four documents in one binding, each self-contained at its own depth: the Field Guide (usable without the theory), the Academic Paper (refutable, with the evidence graded honestly), the Coherence Codex (the drop-in governance stack — also on the toolkits shelf), and the full Manuscript v2.0. For the reader who wants the whole stack. Asks a real read — days, not minutes.
  • Operations Manual — Field Edition v2.0 · June 2026 · conforms to Invariant v2.0 · ~63pp · markdown source The practice as a manual instead of an argument — how to run this on your own ground, theory stripped to what operates. For someone who wants to use it, not study it. Asks reference use: open it where your problem is, not at page one.

The working lens

  • The Meta-Tetrahedron · May 2026 · 8 parts The current operating lens, walked end to end: four capacities, six tensions between them, four ways it fails, one whole. The geometry the rest of the shelf keeps pointing at. For the reader who wants the shape first. Asks a sitting or two; twelve diagrams carry half the load.
  • Visual references · May 2026 · 14 visuals The same recognitions as one-glance diagrams — each one passes a five-second test. For the reader who sees structure before they read it. Asks five seconds each.
  • The walkable tetrahedron The geometry as a thing you turn with your hands — an interactive model of the whole shape, edges and faces clickable. For the reader who learns by handling. Asks a few minutes of play.

Framework texts

One idea per text, taken all the way down. Each is readable alone.

  • A Theory of Gift Why giving without a price isn't charity or foolishness but a working economic form — read through how this site itself runs. For anyone the Model left wanting the why.
  • Consent Architecture The claim that a real "no" is load-bearing structure, not politeness — and what it means to build so the no is always findable. For anyone who's been in a group where the yes was hollow.
  • Recognition Threshold Why good work has to be picked up to count, and what determines whether it is. For anyone who's made something true and watched it go unused.
  • Refusal As A Generative Act The long version of the Refusals page: how saying no builds things. For the reader who wants the structure under that page's depth layer.
  • The Invisible Infrastructure of a Village What carries a community without being seen by it, the five conditions that keep it alive, and what kills it. For anyone trying to grow or guard a real place.
  • Building Infrastructure for Yourself The body-first ground under all of it: permission is something you cross by acting, not something you're argued into. For the reader who keeps waiting to feel ready.
  • Regulatory Aikido Working with structural force instead of against it — four moves, four ways it goes wrong. For anyone facing a system bigger than they are.

Case studies

  • Seven Case Portraits The same pattern read in seven real places — Las Gaviotas, CouchSurfing, partner dance, open-source hardware, two intentional economies, Wikipedia — including the one that failed, on purpose. For the reader who trusts examples over theory.
  • Twelve Failure-Mode Patterns Twelve ways groups break, three per missing capacity, each with what it looks like and what actually fixes it. For diagnosing a group you're inside of. Pairs with the toolkits.

  • Canonical Syntheses · eight documents · ~40,000 words The operational layer for someone installing this framework on their own work — operating principles, cartography, trust generation, onboarding, recomposition. The most technical register on the shelf; it assumes you've read the rest. Asks serious study.

The older shape of this site

This site used to lead with the framework and keep the life behind it; now it's the other way around. The older rooms still stand, unchanged: the archive note that maps them, and the operational pages (consultation tiers, the donation rail) the practice ran on. Old links still work. Nothing was deleted — it was turned inside out.
