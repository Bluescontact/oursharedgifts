# Pull-Quotes — Building Infrastructure for Yourself

Five standalone share-cards. Each holds without context, in Kevin's register.

---

## 1 — The bottleneck

Permission, not skill, is the bottleneck. The body crosses the threshold or it doesn't. Argument cannot cross it; only acting at the threshold can.

---

## 2 — The body is the discriminator

When a tool, framework, or arrangement passes the logical test but the body says no, the body is right. The cognitive system can rationalize anything. The nervous system can't.

---

## 3 — The inversion

The proof arrives by being the proof, not by being convinced first. Waiting for permission keeps the threshold uncrossable, because the permission was never going to arrive by argument.

---

## 4 — Full rigor

Full rigor is not perfection. It is the refusal to build at a lower standard because the work is "only for you."

---

## 5 — The declarative form

I live here. And I built this. And it works. And I did it like it mattered.
