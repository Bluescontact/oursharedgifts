# Read-notes — Seven Case Portraits

## Load-bearing distinction(s)

1. **One form, seven angles.** The primary structural claim: the seven cases are not seven separate things — they are one underlying form (the four-vertex meta-tetrahedron) read from seven angles. Each case surfaces a different framework move (a vertex, an edge, a scale, a failure), but the form is single. The cases emanate from the form; the form is the source.

2. **Case portrait, not case study** (secondary). A portrait names what is structurally visible without arguing the framework's claim; the case does its own work. A case study argues from a remove and must defend a position. This is a register distinction, not the shape of the whole piece.

3. **Including failure.** CouchSurfing is named explicitly — structural proxy preserved, substrate not. A set of only successes is a celebratory list, not a structural read. The failure case is load-bearing.

## Chosen geometry

**source-and-emanation** (extended to seven emanation nodes). One luminous source at center = the form; seven nodes radiating out = the seven cases as angles on it. This is the only geometry whose shape matches the article's actual argument: a single form expressing across instances, substrates, and scales. (A tetrahedron would name the four vertices, not the seven-cases-from-one-form move; vertex-mapped would imply the cases are positions ON the figure rather than instances OF it.)

## Naming phrase

**SEVEN ANGLES ON ONE FORM**
