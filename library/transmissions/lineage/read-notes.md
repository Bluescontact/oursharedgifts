# Read-notes — Lineage

## Load-bearing distinction(s)

- **Synthesis vs. substrate.** The framework is the visible synthesis at the surface; what it rests on is older than the synthesis. Naming the lineage is admitting the substrate predates the form.
- Secondary (held in subtext, not the pattern): **lineage as substrate vs. lineage as bibliography/credential.** The page is a structural map of what each contribution *gave*, not an alphabetized list of names invoked for authority.

## Chosen geometry

**Layered substrate.** The argument is depths beneath a surface: the framework as the top stratum, the contributing strands as the layers it metabolized, and ancestral/Indigenous practice as the deepest ground that predates the academic synthesis. The shape of the page is literally a downward stack from named synthesis to older living traditions — layered substrate is the only template that renders that descent.

## Naming phrase

**LINEAGE AS SUBSTRATE** — subtitle: *the synthesis rests on ground older than itself.*
