# Read-notes — Glossary

## Load-bearing distinction(s)

The page opens on its own hinge: **framework usage vs common usage**. A term that means what common usage means is not yet structurally specific; when it means something different, the difference is load-bearing — the divergence-line *is* the framework move the term names.

But the deeper structural fact is that the glossary is not alphabetized — it is **organized by the parts of one figure**. The vocabulary is grouped by structural relation: four vertices (capacities), six edges (integrations), four faces (failure modes), one center. The 4·6·4·1 form is not one entry among many; it is the spine the whole vocabulary hangs on. Every term is a part of that single geometry, or a concept that operates across it.

## Chosen geometry

**tetrahedron / minimum system.** The glossary's section headings literally walk the tetrahedron: vertices → edges → faces → center. The figure that names the page is the figure the page is built out of. A distinction template would catch only the framework/common-usage hinge and miss that the entire vocabulary is one form decomposed.
