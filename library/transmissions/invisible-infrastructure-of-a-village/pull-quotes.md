# Pull-Quotes — The Invisible Infrastructure of a Village

Standalone share-cards. Each stands without context.

---

## 1

The visible infrastructure is the smaller portion. The larger portion is invisible — who does what for whom, who carries what, who shows up when. The invisible part is what the place actually is.

---

## 2

Most of what the village runs on is not committed to artifact — because committing it to artifact would destroy the form that carries it.

---

## 3

A village whose visible infrastructure is intact but whose invisible infrastructure has eroded looks fine from outside and is hollow from inside.

---

## 4

None of this appears in any formal map. None of it is governance. None of it is exchangeable. All of it is load-bearing.

---

## 5

Counting them destroys them. Documenting them formalizes them. Turning them into a model invites the extraction that follows model-attention. Villages that resist becoming examples of villages are the ones that remain villages.
