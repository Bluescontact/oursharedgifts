# Read-notes — The Four Substrates

## Load-bearing distinction(s)

- **Form vs. ground.** The four-vertex pattern (Differentiation, Connection, Boundaries, Architecture) is the *form*. The substrate (biological, institutional, relational, technical) is the *ground* the form runs in. The piece insists these are not the same thing: the geometry is invariant, the medium varies.
- **Rows vs. columns.** "Read the rows, not the columns." The vertex pattern is what repeats; the substrate is only where the pattern's medium changes. The recognition is the *isomorphism* — the same strategic move (withdrawal that deposits a gift at the boundary) recurring across four grounds, each demanding its own medium (embodied / picked up / speakable / executable).
- **The medium is part of the gift.** A gift left in the wrong medium is not picked up. An idea about how to feel better is not biological substrate; a wiki page that needs interpretation is not technical substrate.

## Chosen geometry

**tetrahedron / minimum system.** The article is the four-vertex form recognized as invariant across changing ground. The tetrahedron is that form — the four functional roles that must hold simultaneously (not stages, not a hierarchy). The four substrates are named as the grounds the same figure repeats across. One figure, four grounds: the isomorphism made visible. (Rejected: *layered* reads as depths-beneath-a-surface, which is the wrong relation — the substrates are parallel, not stacked. *distinction* and *threshold* collapse a four-fold form into a two-fold one.)

## Naming phrase

**The Four Substrates** — subtitle: *Same form. Different ground.*

## Pull-quote candidates

1. The geometry is the same. The medium changes.
2. Read the rows, not the columns. The vertex pattern is what repeats.
3. The four vertices are not stages or hierarchies. They are four roles the form must hold at once.
4. A gift left in the wrong medium is not picked up. It cannot do its work. The medium is part of the gift.
5. The same move — withdrawal that deposits a gift at the boundary — uses different media in every substrate.
