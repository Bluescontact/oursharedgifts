# Pull-Quotes — Naming Bridge

Standalone share-cards. Each holds without the article around it.

---

## 1

An idea that can only be named philosophically is not yet grounded. An idea that can only be named structurally is not yet operational. An idea that can only be named operationally has lost the form that lets it propagate.

---

## 2

The naming bridge is the practice of always being able to translate the same idea across all three registers — and reading the slip when one register is missing.

---

## 3

Two registers collapse the structural layer. Without structure, theory and practice become opposed rather than translatable; the bridge becomes a chasm.

---

## 4

A practitioner whose work lives in only one register cannot teach it, propagate it, or stress-test it.

---

## 5

When the bridge slips, the structural register has usually gone missing. Structure is what holds the other two in alignment.
