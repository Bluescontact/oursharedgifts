# Pull-quotes — Regulatory Aikido

Standalone share-cards. Each holds without context. Render text-only, warm bone on near-black, refined serif.

---

## 1

A system designed to absorb opposition will absorb opposition. The move is not to push harder, but to find the pressure point where a small precise intervention produces change the system's own momentum carries forward.

---

## 2

Force met with force tests which side has more force. The result is exhaustion on both sides, with the underlying structure largely intact.

---

## 3

The pressure point is rarely the symptom. It is the structural element the symptom is downstream of — often boring, technical, and out of public view. That is why it is a pressure point.

---

## 4

The system cannot refuse the intervention without refusing its own legitimacy.

---

## 5

Working with the system's own logic in service of structural integrity is aikido. Using that same logic to extract from it is manipulation. The body knows the difference.
