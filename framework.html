The framework is a tetrahedron with four vertices, six edges, four faces, and one held center. Each vertex names a capacity. Each edge names how two capacities relate. Each face names what fails when one vertex is missing. The center is the somatic ground the figure rests on.

Most positions are visibly held — substrate composting, surface arriving when ripe. The hold is the discipline, not the deficiency.

Center · somatic ground · last to be built

The body the figure rests on

The four vertices, the six edges, and the four faces together describe a figure in space. The center is what the figure is built around and what holds it when the figure is under load.

For Kevin the center is the body. The bus. The biochar toilet. The forty-five mile radius around Grass Valley. The four-hour shared-meal threshold. Building Infrastructure for Yourself — the somatic-permission essay underneath the whole operating system.

Articulation and presentation arrive last. The center holds because the body holds. Building it before the edges and faces have settled would invert the discipline.
