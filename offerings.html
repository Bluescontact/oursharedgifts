What I Do

Everything here is a gift. Invite me into what you need.

This isn't a menu of services, and I'm not running a business. It's an open door. If something here fits what you're carrying, invite me in and I'll show up and do the work. It's offered as a gift — the circle completes through recognition, not payment. You won't walk away owing me. A donation or a good word can flow back if you want it to, but it's never the price of asking; I don't negotiate and I don't ask for references. The edges are plain so you can ask clean.

With my hands

Twenty years of trade skills behind them. I build, I fix, I weld, I clear a space out and put it back together so it works.

Off-grid systems

  • Solar — about 2.2 kW, battery, inverter
  • Water — pump-and-tank, pressure on demand
  • Bus and rig-out builds, put to your setup

This is the work I know cold, because I run my own off it. The bus is my house and my generator — about 2.2 kilowatts of solar (six 440-watt panels), a 10 kW split-phase inverter, a battery bank, and a 12V pump with an accumulator tank I built that gives the place running water on demand. Built mostly out of salvage, runs free now, expanding toward ten panels. I can help you set up the same kind of thing — or, if you'd rather do it yourself, the whole build is written down and given away in The Model.

Welding & repairs

  • Welding
  • Fixing what's broken
  • The kind of build one person plus your hands can finish in the time we agree on

Building & wood work

  • Shelves, planter boxes, small furniture, simple repairs
  • A custom piece if it's something I can do well

I work in wood too, and I'll be straight about it: it's something I'm building up, not a furniture shop. Small, real, what a clear scope and one pair of hands can finish. If you've got something bigger in mind, ask and we'll talk plainly about what it'd take.

Cleaning & organizing

  • A room, a garage, a vehicle, a closet, a desk cleared and sorted by how it actually gets used

The clearing and the structure are what I bring. What you keep is your call.

On money, plainly

There's no price list and nothing to haggle over. If you want to put something in — a donation, you pick the number — that's welcome and never required; it doesn't buy the work and it doesn't settle a debt, because there isn't one. The one practical thing: before I take on anything that needs a permit or a license, I check what's legal where you are first. Permits are the difference between a real plan and a wish.

With my attention

The same door, a different kind of support. No string, no ledger, no "you owe me one" — same as everything else here. Take what's useful, leave the rest.

Witnessing

An hour or so of attention with no agenda. You talk; I hold the listening. What comes back is what I heard — the pattern under what you said, the question under the question. No fixing, no advice unless you ask, no notes kept after.

Thinking partner

An hour or so of thinking out loud on a real problem you bring. I reflect it back, name what I see, ask the question you're not asking yourself. The thinking is the gift. The problem stays yours.

Partner dance

An hour or so of partnered movement — lead and follow, frame, weight, the feel of moving with someone. Twenty years of it, mostly Fusion. The social kind, not the competition kind. No performances, no wedding routines.

If what you need isn't here

Ask anyway. The list is a starting point, not a fence. Some of what people ask for fits one of these framed a little differently. Some of it belongs to the farm or the gathering more than to me in particular — that goes to the Gift Circle. And some things I don't do — that's information, not judgment.
