Offerings

What Kevin gives in the gift zone.

Five offerings. Each one names what is offered, what is not offered, and the Wheel of Consent quadrant it operates in. The negative space is a structural fact, not a disclaimer — what isn't on the menu protects what is.

No prices. No scarcity. No Book Now. If something fits, ask about it.

Read slowly. Notice which one settles in your chest, and which one your mind reaches for. They will rarely be the same.

Witnessing

Offered
Sixty to ninety minutes of attention without agenda. You speak; Kevin holds the structure of listening. What gets returned is what was heard — patterns the speaker did not narrate, the question underneath the question.
Not offered
No fixing. No advice unless you ask. No therapy. No analysis you did not request. No notes kept after the conversation closes.

Thinking partner

Offered
Sixty to ninety minutes of structured thinking on a specific problem you bring. Kevin reflects, names patterns, asks the question you are not asking yourself. The thinking is the gift; the problem stays yours.
Not offered
Not the consultation tier — that exists separately, with named scope and sliding-scale exchange. No deliverable, no plan you can hand off. No follow-up sessions unless mutually offered.

Cleaning & organizing

Offered
A specific space cleared, sorted, and ordered by how it actually gets used — a room, a garage, a vehicle, a closet, a desk, a folder of digital files. The clearing and the structure are what Kevin contributes; the decisions about what to keep are yours.
First session
Thirty minutes to walk the space and agree the goal and scope before any work begins. The work is scoped from there and finished within the window agreed.
Not offered
No ongoing service or maintenance after the agreed scope. No decisions about what to keep or discard — those stay yours. No therapeutic framing on what your space reflects, no critique of its state. No branded methodology. No specialty work that requires a licensed service (mold, biohazard, hoarding intervention).

Building

Offered
A specific small build — a shelf, a planter box, a small piece of furniture, a simple repair. Two decades of trades work, the bus build, the biochar toilet, and the tools that came from them, applied to what one pair of hands and a clear scope can complete.
Not offered
Not a contractor service. No structural work, no licensed work, no permitted work. No projects that exceed what one person plus your hands can finish in the agreed time. Materials are yours to supply; Kevin brings tools and labor.

Partner dance instruction

Offered
Sixty to ninety minutes of partnered movement work — lead and follow mechanics, frame, weight transfer, the feel of co-regulation in motion. Twenty years of partner dance, primarily Fusion Dancing; the social register, not the competition register.
Not offered
No competition coaching. No performance preparation. No "we'll learn five songs for our wedding." Mutual movement work, not a class structure with curriculum.

About the quadrant labels All five offerings sit in Serving — Kevin doing, you benefiting from what Kevin does. The Wheel of Consent (Serving · Allowing · Taking · Accepting) is the substrate this framing rests on. Naming the quadrant makes the structural shape of each offering visible at intake; misreading it produces the shadow forms the framework refuses. Source: Consent Architecture Synthesis, in the Library.

If what you need isn't here

Ask anyway. The list is a starting point, not a fence. Some asks turn out to fit one of the six framed differently. Some are outside the gift zone — those route to consultations (sliding-scale, exchange tier) or to a different practitioner Kevin can name. Some are a better fit for the local fabric than for Kevin in particular — those go to the Gift Circle, where asks pair with offers within the radius. Some are refused by design — that's information, not judgment.

Capacity is body-discriminated, not slot-counted. See Current State for what's open right now.
