Open Source Canon

These 35 patterns were built from lived experience across safety work, labor organizing, economic systems, and the structural mechanics of how knowledge moves between people. They are released completely — no license, no terms, no requirement to cite or credit.

Use them. Develop them. Test them in your territory. Fork the library and build your own version. The patterns that survive across multiple territories are the ones that are actually structural — not contextual to one person's life. Your territory is the test.

How to Use the Patterns

1

Read the pattern

Each pattern has a one-move description and a development section showing the mechanism. Start with the one-move statement — if it doesn't name something you've already experienced, the pattern may not be active in your territory yet.

2

Test it against a real situation

The pattern is useful if it gives you a coordinate — a place to stand that makes the structure navigable. If it just describes what's happening without changing your orientation, it hasn't landed yet.

3

Note what holds and what doesn't

Most patterns will hold partially. Note the conditions under which they hold in your territory — that's the most useful development work. The background conditions that make a pattern work are often invisible until they're absent.

4

Develop in your direction

Fork the library. Build your own version calibrated to your field. The form is transferable; the calibration is yours. A nurse's version of the Consented Ledger looks different from a safety engineer's — both are real.

Contributing Back

If you test a pattern and find that it holds, fails, or extends in your territory — that's a contribution. The simplest form: describe where it held and where it broke. The canon gets stronger from cross-territory confirmation, not from more material being generated in one territory.

→

Open a pull request

The pattern library is open source at github.com/Bluescontact/oursharedgifts. A PR with your territory notes — what held, what didn't, what background conditions you found — is the contribution form. Kevin reviews at the gate; the Converger screens first.

→

Write to Kevin directly

Use the three-part ask form from The Door. If you've been working with the patterns in a specific territory and want to discuss what you've found, that's what the door is for.

The Geometric Loop

Each cycle of diverse intake strengthens the canon. A stronger canon routes more accurately in the next cycle. More accurate routing means less noise at the gate — which means Kevin can hold more contributions without the gate becoming a burden. More contributions from more territories means more confirmation — which makes the canon stronger.

This is not additive. It's geometric. The canon doesn't just grow — it becomes more real. The difference between a pattern confirmed in one territory and a pattern confirmed across five is the difference between a personal observation and structural law.
