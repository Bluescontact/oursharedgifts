Aquaponic Greenhouse

STATUS: Active Development | Location: Laguna, NM

1-acre demonstration site integrating aquaponics production, permanent pond infrastructure, food forest establishment, and wind protection systems. Designed for rapid biodiversity generation using holistic and systems-based design in New Mexico high desert conditions.

System Overview

This project demonstrates complete ecosystem integration on a single acre:

  • Aquaponics greenhouse producing food while generating nutrient-rich water
  • Permanent pond for thermal mass, water storage, and aquaculture
  • Food forest establishing perennial production around pond
  • Wind protection using excavated material and living systems
  • Closed-loop nutrient cycling between all components

Site Conditions & Constraints

LOCATION

Laguna, New Mexico

ZONE

6a (-10°F to -5°F)

PRECIPITATION

10-14" annually

WIND

Intense, sustained

SOIL

Heavy clay, alkaline

ELEVATION

~6,000 feet

System Components

1. Aquaponics Greenhouse

Design approach: Recirculating water system with fish providing nutrients for plant production. All water cycles through greenhouse, pond, and food forest.

Core System Specifications

  • Water volume: 5,000-10,000 gallons total system
  • Growing beds: 20mil Durascrim liner in elevated beds
  • Fish progression: Establish with feeder goldfish, transition to koi
  • Monitoring: 24/7 automated sensors (pH, DO, EC, temperature)
  • Power: Solar with battery backup for critical systems

Why This Approach

  • Feeder fish first: Test system stability with $50 of fish before $5,000 koi investment
  • Elevated beds: Ground-level installation simplifies flood/drain, prevents liner penetration
  • Durascrim liner: Alkaline soil protection without excavation costs
  • Comprehensive monitoring: Pattern recognition enables predictive management, documents for replication

2. Permanent Pond System

Design: Long shallow wedge pond with planting ledges, integrated with aquaponics overflow and food forest water needs.

Pond Specifications

  • Depth: 8-10 feet maximum (below frost line)
  • Planting ledges: 12 inches deep, 4 feet wide on both sides
  • Shape: Wedge profile for habitat diversity
  • Function: Thermal mass, water storage, ecosystem establishment, aquaculture
  • Excavation use: Dirt becomes wind protection berms

Ecosystem Function

  • Marginal plants (cattails, rushes, water iris) on 12-inch ledges filter nutrients and create habitat
  • Deep end stays oxygenated in summer, prevents total freeze in winter
  • Humidity microclimate benefits surrounding food forest plantings
  • Insect control: Frog and dragonfly habitat reduces greenhouse pests
  • Thermal mass: Moderates temperature extremes for nearby plantings

3. Food Forest Integration

Design: Perennial polyculture establishing around pond, receiving aquaponics overflow water and nutrients.

Planting Zones

  • Inner ring (pond edge): Water-tolerant species, humidity-loving plants
  • Middle zone: Fruit trees tapping pond water table
  • Outer ring: Berries, shrubs, groundcover establishing windbreak
  • Irrigation: Drip system fed from aquaponics overflow

Species Selection Criteria

  • Zone 6a hardy: Must survive -10°F winters
  • Wind tolerant: High desert sustained wind conditions
  • Water efficient: Thrive with aquaponics overflow but tolerate dry periods
  • Multiple functions: Food production + wildlife habitat + windbreak

4. Wind Protection Strategy

Approach: Layered protection using excavated berms, living windbreaks, and strategic placement.

Wind Management

  • Excavated berms: Pond spoil creates immediate wind break
  • Prevailing wind: SW primary, N secondary
  • Berm positioning: Largest on SW side, funnel effect over greenhouse
  • Living windbreak: Fast-growing species (four-wing saltbush, Russian olive)
  • Soil protection: Immediate planting prevents berm erosion

System Integration: How Everything Connects

Closed-Loop Nutrient Flow

FISH (koi) → WASTE → BACTERIA (nitrification) → NITRATES →
GREENHOUSE PLANTS (uptake) → CLEAN WATER →
POND (storage/thermal mass) → FOOD FOREST (irrigation) →
INFILTRATION → GROUNDWATER → back to SYSTEM

Energy Flows

  • Solar → electricity: Powers pumps, aeration, monitoring
  • Sun → thermal mass: Pond moderates temperature extremes
  • Wind → mechanical: Berms deflect, living systems convert to biomass
  • Fish waste → nutrients: Fertilizer for all plant systems

Water Flows

  • Aquaponics circulation: Fish tanks → grow beds → filtration → back to tanks
  • Overflow to pond: Excess water → thermal storage → ecosystem function
  • Pond to food forest: Drip irrigation from stored water
  • Catchment: Greenhouse roof → storage → system makeup water

Why Document Freely

This demonstration site generates three outputs:

  1. Food production (transactional baseline)
  2. Living dataset (pattern recognition in action)
  3. Teaching infrastructure (gift to future builders)

The monitoring system isn't just operational necessity—it's documentation infrastructure that makes knowledge transferable without requiring belief. The data speaks for itself. The patterns become visible to anyone willing to look.

This is gift economy embodied in physical infrastructure: value that multiplies through circulation. Take the designs. Adapt them to your climate. Document what you learn. Share improvements back to the commons.
